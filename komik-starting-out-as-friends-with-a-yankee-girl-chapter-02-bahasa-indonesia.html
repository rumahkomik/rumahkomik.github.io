3. 2. Our parents let us …. our friends tonight. a. hang out with b. hung out with c. to hang out with d. hanging out with


kalimat tersebut, jawaban yang tepat adalah "a. hang out with".

Pilihan "b. hung out with" adalah bentuk lampau dari kata kerja "hang out", yang tidak sesuai dengan kalimat yang diberikan. Pilihan "c. to hang out with" adalah konstruksi yang tidak diperlukan dalam kalimat tersebut. Pilihan "d. hanging out with" adalah bentuk gerund yang tidak cocok dalam kalimat tersebut karena kalimat membutuhkan bentuk infinitif "hang out with" untuk menyatakan tindakan yang akan dilakukan.

Jadi, jawaban yang paling tepat adalah "a. hang out with" yang sesuai dengan kalimat "Our parents let us hang out with our friends tonight." yang berarti "Orangtua kita membiarkan kita berkumpul dengan teman-teman kita malam ini."

The correct answer is (a) hang out with. This is because the verb hang out is a phrasal verb that means “to spend time with someone” or “to socialize with someone”. When we use a phrasal verb with an object, we need to put the object after the preposition. For example:

I like to hang out with my friends on weekends.

She hangs out with her cousins every summer.

The other options are incorrect for different reasons:

(b) hung out with is the past tense form of hang out with, but the sentence is in the present tense. The past tense form would be used to talk about something that happened in the past. For example:

Our parents let us hung out with our friends last night.

He hung out with his classmates after school.

(c) to hang out with is the infinitive form of hang out with, but the sentence does not need an infinitive after the verb let. The verb let is followed by an object and a bare infinitive (an infinitive without to). For example:

Our parents let us go to the movies.

She lets him drive her car.

(d) hanging out with is the present participle form of hang out with, but the sentence does not need a present participle after the verb let. The present participle form is used to form continuous tenses or to modify nouns. For example:

We are hanging out with our friends right now.

He is a fun person to hang out with.

32. 1. we use an with word starting....2. we use a with word starting....​


Answer:

An: A, I, E, O, U or if the word starts with a vowel sound

A: With consonant sounds (b,c,d,f,g,h,etc...)

Words Example:

An:

1. An apple (starts with A I E O U and sounds like a vowel)

2. An Honest Person (Doesn't start with A I E O U but when you pronounce it, sounds like a vowel. The H is "Honest" is silent.)

A:

1. A Car (Consonant)

2. A Laptop (Consonant)

Sorry if some are incorrect.
